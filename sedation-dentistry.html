Your Options

Three Levels of Sedation. You Choose Your Comfort.

Every patient is different. We offer three sedation options so we can match the right level of relaxation to your anxiety, your procedure, and your preferences.

😊

Nitrous Oxide

Laughing gas the mildest option. Inhaled through a small mask, it takes effect in minutes and wears off within minutes of removal.

Mild anxiety · Quick recovery
  • Fully conscious, just relaxed
  • Safe to drive yourself home after
  • Great for mild anxiety or kids
  • Easily adjusted during the procedure
💤

IV Sedation

The deepest option delivered intravenously for immediate, fully controlled relaxation. You'll be in a sleep-like state with continuous monitoring throughout.

Most popular · Deepest relaxation
  • Deeply sedated feels like sleep
  • Ideal for extensive or surgical work
  • Instantly adjustable by your doctor
  • Continuous vital sign monitoring
💊

Oral Sedation

A prescription pill taken before your appointment. You'll arrive already relaxed drowsy but conscious and remember little to nothing of the procedure.

Moderate anxiety · No needle
  • Taken the night before and/or morning of
  • You'll need a driver home
  • Deeper relaxation than nitrous
  • Most patients have little memory afterward

Not sure which level is right for you? Just call us and ask we'll talk through it.

Sedation Dentistry FAQs

It depends on the type of sedation. With nitrous oxide, you're fully awake but relaxed. With oral sedation, you're drowsy and responsive but deeply calm. Your Safety Comes First

Sedation Safety & Transparency

Provider credentials: Dr. George F. Johnson III, DMD (Tennessee Dental License #8571) holds a comprehensive sedation permit issued by the Tennessee Board of Dentistry, authorizing the administration of nitrous oxide, oral conscious sedation, and intravenous (IV) sedation. All sedation team members maintain current ACLS (Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support) certification.

Monitoring protocols: During every sedation procedure, patients are continuously monitored using pulse oximetry (blood oxygen levels), non-invasive blood pressure monitoring, capnography (CO2 monitoring for IV sedation), and electrocardiography (heart rhythm). A dedicated team member monitors vitals throughout the entire procedure.

Emergency preparedness: Emergency reversal agents including flumazenil (benzodiazepine reversal) and naloxone (opioid reversal) are immediately available at both locations. Emergency oxygen delivery systems, suction equipment, and a full emergency drug kit are maintained and inspected regularly per ADA and Tennessee Board of Dentistry guidelines.

Potential risks: As with any medical procedure, sedation dentistry carries some risks. These may include nausea, drowsiness, headache, or temporary changes in blood pressure. Rare but serious risks include allergic reaction, respiratory depression, or cardiovascular complications. All risks are discussed during your pre-sedation evaluation, and a written consent form is reviewed and signed before any sedation is administered.

Not every patient is a candidate. Certain health conditions, medications, or allergies may make sedation inadvisable. Your medical history is carefully reviewed before any sedation is recommended. We will always recommend the safest approach for your individual situation.
