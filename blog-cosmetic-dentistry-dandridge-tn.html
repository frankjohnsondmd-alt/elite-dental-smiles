What Counts as Cosmetic Dentistry?

Cosmetic dentistry refers to any dental procedure whose primary purpose is to improve the appearance of your teeth, gums, or overall smile. It overlaps with restorative dentistry in many cases. A dental crown, for example, restores a broken tooth but also improves its appearance. A veneer placed over a discolored tooth is cosmetic. Whitening is purely cosmetic.

The distinction matters because it affects insurance coverage. Most dental insurance plans do not cover procedures that are considered purely cosmetic. Procedures that restore damaged teeth may qualify for partial coverage. We will clarify this at your consultation so there are no surprises.

The Most Common Cosmetic Procedures We Offer

Professional Teeth Whitening

Whitening is the most requested cosmetic procedure in dentistry, and for good reason. It is the fastest, least invasive, and most affordable way to make a visible improvement in how your smile looks. Professional whitening uses a stronger bleaching concentration than anything available over the counter, which means faster results and more even coverage.

At Elite Dental Smiles, we offer both in-office whitening and take-home custom trays. In-office whitening produces results in a single appointment, typically one to two shades lighter than your starting shade. Take-home trays use a lower concentration applied over one to two weeks and can produce similar or even better results with a bit more patience.

Whitening works on natural tooth enamel. It does not change the color of crowns, veneers, or composite bonding. If you have restorations on your front teeth, we will talk about sequencing your treatment so everything matches.

Dental Bonding

Bonding uses tooth-colored composite resin applied directly to the tooth surface to fix chips, close small gaps, cover stains, or reshape teeth that look uneven. It is done in a single appointment with no lab wait. In most cases, no anesthesia is needed.

The results are immediate and look natural when done well. The main trade-off compared to veneers is durability. Bonding lasts 5 to 10 years and is more prone to staining over time. It is the right choice for targeted fixes on one or two teeth when you want a conservative, affordable result. For a more comprehensive transformation, veneers are usually worth the additional investment.

Porcelain Veneers

Veneers are thin porcelain shells custom fabricated in a dental lab and bonded permanently to the front surface of your teeth. They are the gold standard for comprehensive cosmetic changes to front teeth. Veneers can transform the shape, size, color, and alignment of your smile in a way that looks completely natural and lasts 15 to 20 years with proper care.

The trade-off is that placing veneers requires removing a small amount of enamel from the front of each tooth, which means the process is permanent. The teeth need to be covered indefinitely once treated. For patients who are committed to the result and want the most durable cosmetic option available, veneers consistently deliver.

We offer porcelain veneers at our Dandridge location. A consultation appointment lets us take photos and discuss the smile design before any commitment is made.

Clear Aligners

If your primary concern is the alignment of your teeth rather than their color or shape, clear aligners offer a discreet way to straighten teeth without traditional metal braces. We provide aligner treatment at Elite Dental Smiles for adults and older teens who want to correct crowding, spacing, or mild bite issues.

Clear aligner treatment typically takes 6 to 18 months depending on complexity. Aligners are removable, which means no food restrictions and easier cleaning compared to fixed braces. The result is straighter teeth and, in many cases, a better foundation for any additional cosmetic work you want to do afterward.

Dental Crowns for Cosmetic Purposes

Crowns are most often placed to restore teeth that are cracked, heavily decayed, or broken. But they also serve cosmetic purposes when a tooth is too damaged or discolored for bonding or a veneer to cover effectively. A crown completely caps the tooth and can dramatically improve its appearance while also restoring full function. Crowns are made from porcelain, ceramic, or a combination with metal, and are matched to the color of your surrounding teeth.

Smile Makeovers

A smile makeover is not a single procedure. It is a coordinated treatment plan that combines multiple cosmetic procedures to comprehensively transform your smile. A typical smile makeover might include whitening the back teeth, placing veneers on the front six teeth, and using bonding to adjust a slightly mismatched tooth nearby. The plan is built around your goals, your timeline, and your budget.

At our Dandridge office, we take time with these consultations because getting the plan right matters more than rushing into treatment. We look at your tooth color, shape, proportion, the way your teeth show when you smile and when you talk, your gum line, and how everything fits together. The goal is a result that looks natural, not overdone.

What Does Cosmetic Dentistry Cost in Dandridge?

Cost is the most common question we get, and the honest answer is that it varies significantly by procedure and by how many teeth are involved. Here is a general range to help you plan:

Dental bonding: $200 to $600 per tooth. A single chipped front tooth is typically on the lower end of that range.

How to Choose the Right Cosmetic Procedure

The most useful thing you can do before a cosmetic consultation is identify specifically what bothers you about your smile. Is it the color? The shape of a particular tooth? The way your teeth look when you smile in photos? Alignment? The gum line? The more specific you can be, the better we can match a solution to the actual problem.

We also look at the health of your teeth and gums before recommending any cosmetic work. Whitening on a tooth with untreated decay will cause sensitivity. Veneers on teeth with active gum disease will not last. Good cosmetic results are built on a healthy foundation, so we address any health issues first and then move to the cosmetic work.

Common Questions About Cosmetic Dentistry

How much does cosmetic dentistry cost in Dandridge, TN?

Costs vary widely depending on the procedure. Teeth whitening starts around $300 to $500 for professional in-office treatment. Dental bonding runs $200 to $600 per tooth. Porcelain veneers range from $1,000 to $2,000 per tooth. A full smile makeover combining multiple procedures can range from $3,000 to $15,000 or more depending on complexity. We offer consultations to help you understand your options and prioritize based on your budget.

Does dental insurance cover cosmetic dentistry?

Most dental insurance plans do not cover purely cosmetic procedures such as teeth whitening or veneers. However, some procedures that also have functional benefits, such as dental bonding to repair a chipped tooth or a crown to restore a broken tooth, may be partially covered. Our Dandridge office is fee for service and does not accept insurance, which allows us to focus entirely on the best treatment for you. Our Jefferson City office accepts most dental insurance plans.

What is the best cosmetic dental procedure for my smile?

The best procedure depends on what you want to change. For stained or yellowed teeth, whitening is the most conservative and affordable starting point. For chips, gaps, or minor shape issues, bonding can often fix one or two teeth in a single visit. For a comprehensive transformation of multiple front teeth, veneers deliver the most durable and dramatic results. A cosmetic consultation at Elite Dental Smiles will help us understand your goals and recommend the most efficient path to the smile you want.

How long do cosmetic dental results last?

Results depend on the procedure. Professional teeth whitening lasts 6 to 18 months with touch-ups as needed. Dental bonding lasts 5 to 10 years. Porcelain veneers last 15 to 20 years or more with proper care. Dental crowns used for cosmetic purposes can last 15 years or longer. Maintaining good home care habits, avoiding tobacco, and attending regular cleanings will maximize the life of any cosmetic work.
