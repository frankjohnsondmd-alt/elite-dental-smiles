Know Your Options

Professional vs. Store-Bought: There's No Contest.

Over-the-counter products use low-concentration bleach that works slowly and unevenly. Professional whitening uses prescription-strength gel — and the results show it.

Why Teeth Darken

What Causes Tooth Discoloration?

Most staining is surface-level and responds well to professional whitening. Understanding what caused the discoloration helps set realistic expectations for your results.

☕

Coffee & Tea

Two of the most common culprits. The tannins in coffee and tea bind to tooth enamel and build up over time, yellowing teeth even with regular brushing.

🍷

Red Wine & Dark Drinks

Red wine, dark sodas, and sports drinks all contain chromogens — pigmented compounds that stain enamel. Even moderate consumption adds up over years.

🚬

Tobacco Use

Smoking and chewing tobacco cause some of the deepest, most stubborn staining. Nicotine and tar penetrate enamel and can cause yellow-to-brown discoloration that worsens over time.

⏳

Age & Enamel Thinning

As enamel naturally wears down with age, the yellowish dentin layer underneath shows through more. This type of intrinsic discoloration still responds to whitening in many cases.

💊

Certain Medications

Tetracycline antibiotics (taken during tooth development), antihistamines, and some blood pressure medications can cause internal tooth staining that varies in how well it responds to whitening.

🦷

Dental Trauma

A tooth that was knocked or bumped hard can develop internal discoloration as the pulp reacts. This type of staining is typically treated differently than surface staining.

Are You a Candidate?

Whitening Works Best When…

Professional whitening produces excellent results for most patients with surface staining. Here's who sees the best outcomes.

😬

Your Teeth Are Healthy

Whitening works best on teeth free of cavities, active gum disease, or cracked enamel. We'll check at your appointment and treat anything needed first.

🟡

Your Staining Is Yellow or Brown

Yellow and brown extrinsic stains from food and drinks respond best to whitening. Gray staining from medications or trauma can be more resistant — we'll advise you honestly.

📅

You Have a Special Event Coming Up

Wedding, reunion, job interview, graduation — professional whitening is the fastest way to look your best. In-office whitening can dramatically brighten your smile in a single session.

💰

You Want Affordable Cosmetic Results

Whitening is one of the most cost-effective cosmetic dental treatments available. Before committing to veneers or bonding, most cosmetic dentists recommend whitening first.

Common Questions

Teeth Whitening FAQs

Results vary based on the type and depth of your staining, but most patients see 4–8 shades of improvement with take-home trays and 6–10 shades with in-office whitening. Yellow and brown staining from food and drinks typically responds the most dramatically. We'll give you an honest expectation at your consultation based on your starting shade.
Some patients experience temporary tooth sensitivity during or after whitening — typically described as brief zingers or a generalized achiness. This is normal and usually resolves within 24–48 hours. If you have naturally sensitive teeth, we may recommend a desensitizing treatment beforehand or suggest a milder whitening protocol.
With proper care, professional whitening results typically last 1–3 years. How long depends largely on your habits — coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco will restain teeth faster. Take-home tray patients can easily do periodic touch-ups at home to maintain their results. In-office patients often do a brief take-home touch-up every 6–12 months.
Whitening agents only work on natural tooth enamel — they won't change the shade of crowns, veneers, or composite bonding. This means existing restorations may stand out more after whitening if they don't match your new, brighter shade. We'll review your existing restorations at your consultation and advise whether whitening makes sense for your situation — or whether replacement may be worth considering.
Both produce excellent results — the best option depends on your lifestyle and timeline. In-office whitening delivers the most dramatic result in a single visit, which is ideal if you have an event coming up or prefer a "done in one" experience. Take-home trays cost less, are done at your convenience, and the custom trays are reusable for future touch-ups. Many patients do in-office first for the dramatic jump, then maintain with take-home trays.
