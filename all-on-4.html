The Procedure

What Is All-on-4?

All-on-4 replaces an entire arch of missing or failing teeth using just four strategically placed implants. Unlike traditional full-mouth implants which require one post per tooth All-on-4 is efficient, minimally invasive, and often doesn't require bone grafting even in patients with bone loss.

🔩

The Four Implants

Four titanium posts are placed in the jawbone two upright in the front and two angled in the back. The angulation maximizes contact with existing bone, which is why bone grafting is often not required.

😁

The Full Arch Bridge

A full set of prosthetic teeth a complete arch is attached to the four implants. This bridge is fixed and permanent. It does not come out, does not slip, and does not require adhesive. You brush it like natural teeth.

⚡

Immediate Loading

In many cases, a temporary arch is attached the same day as implant surgery. You leave the office with teeth not a gap. The final permanent arch is placed once healing is complete (typically 3–6 months).

Step by Step

The All-on-4 Process: What to Expect

From your first consultation to your final smile, here's what the journey looks like typically completed in 4–6 months from start to finish.

01

Consultation & 3D Imaging

We perform a comprehensive oral exam including 3D cone beam CT scans to evaluate your jawbone volume, nerve location, and sinus proximity. You'll leave with a complete treatment plan, timeline, and cost estimate no surprises.

1 visit · ~90 min
02

Extractions (If Needed)

If you have remaining failing teeth, they're removed often on the same day as implant placement. We plan this carefully so you're never without teeth. Dr. Johnson is certified in IV sedation, so you can sleep through this entire phase.

Same day as surgery when possible · sedation available
03

Implant Placement & Temporary Arch

All four implants are surgically placed in the jawbone. In most cases, a temporary full-arch prosthesis is attached the same day so you leave the office with a complete set of teeth. Recovery is typically a few days of rest with manageable discomfort.

1 day · sedation available · immediate teeth in many cases
04

Healing & Osseointegration

Over the next 3–6 months, the implants fuse with your jawbone through a natural process called osseointegration. You'll continue wearing your temporary arch during this period. Follow-up visits monitor your healing progress.

3–6 months · healing period
05

Final Arch Placement

Once fully healed, your temporary arch is replaced with your custom-crafted permanent prosthesis designed to match your facial features, lip support, and bite. This is your final result: a complete, permanent, fixed smile.

1–2 visits · final result

Common Questions

All-on-4 FAQs

The surgical day including any extractions, implant placement, and fitting of your temporary arch typically takes 4–6 hours. Most patients are under sedation for this portion. From first consultation to final permanent arch, the complete process takes 4–6 months, with the majority of that time being the healing period between surgery and final placement.
The procedure itself is performed under IV sedation or local anesthesia you won't feel it. Post-surgery, most patients experience swelling, bruising, and soreness for the first 5–7 days, managed with prescribed pain medication. The majority of patients say recovery was more manageable than they expected. By the end of week two, most people are back to normal activities.
In the short term, you'll be on a soft diet for 6–8 weeks while the implants heal. After the healing phase is complete and the final arch is placed, the vast majority of patients can eat normally steak, apples, corn on the cob, everything denture patients have been avoiding for years. Full chewing force is typically restored to around 80–90% of natural teeth.
All-on-4 costs vary depending on how many arches you're treating, whether extractions are needed, and other case-specific factors. We provide a complete, transparent, itemized estimate at your consultation no hidden fees, no pressure. CareCredit financing is available, and when you factor in the 30+ year lifespan of the restoration versus the ongoing replacement cost of dentures, All-on-4 is often the more economical long-term choice.
This is one of the reasons All-on-4 was developed. The rear implants are placed at an angle, allowing them to engage denser bone further back in the jaw bone that typically remains intact even when the front jaw has atrophied. Many patients who were told they need bone grafting for traditional implants are excellent All-on-4 candidates without grafting. We'll evaluate your bone density via 3D imaging at your consultation and give you an honest answer.
Implant-supported dentures (also called snap-on or overdentures) attach to implants but are still removed at night for cleaning. All-on-4 is a fixed, screw-retained arch it does not come out. It's cleaned in your mouth like natural teeth. For patients who want the closest possible experience to real teeth, All-on-4 is the preferred solution. Snap-on dentures are a lower-cost alternative that still offers significant improvement over conventional dentures.
