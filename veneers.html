The Basics

What Are Porcelain Veneers?

Porcelain veneers are ultra-thin ceramic shells about 0.5mm thick custom-crafted and permanently bonded to the front surface of your teeth. The result: a complete smile transformation with minimal tooth alteration.

💎

The Shell

Made from high-strength dental porcelain, each veneer is approximately 0.5mm thin about the thickness of a contact lens. The translucency of porcelain mimics natural tooth enamel perfectly.

🔧

The Preparation

A minimal amount of enamel is gently removed to create room for the veneer. This ensures a natural fit without making your teeth look bulky. In some cases, no-prep veneers are an option.

🔗

The Bond

Each veneer is cemented with a permanent dental-grade adhesive and cured with a special light. Once bonded, your veneer becomes part of your tooth strong, stable, and built to last over a decade.

What We Can Fix

Smile Flaws That Veneers Correct

Porcelain veneers are one of the most versatile cosmetic treatments available. If you're unhappy with the appearance of your teeth, chances are veneers can help.

☕

Stained or Discolored Teeth

Deep intrinsic stains from coffee, tea, tobacco, or medications that whitening can't reach. Veneers cover them completely with a custom-matched shade.

💔

Chipped or Cracked Teeth

Minor chips and surface cracks that affect your smile's appearance can be covered seamlessly with a veneer restoring both looks and confidence.

↔️

Gaps Between Teeth

Small diastemas and spaces between teeth can be closed with veneers often without the time commitment of orthodontic treatment.

📈

Slightly Misaligned Teeth

Mild crookedness or rotation can often be corrected cosmetically with veneers giving you a straighter-looking smile without braces or aligners.

↕️

Short or Uneven Teeth

Teeth that are naturally short or uneven can be lengthened and reshaped to create a more balanced, symmetrical smile line.

🗏️

Worn-Down Teeth

Teeth worn from grinding (bruxism) or acid erosion can be restored in appearance with veneers, bringing back length, shape, and a healthy glow.

Step by Step

The Veneer Process: What to Expect

Most veneer cases are complete in two visits over two to three weeks. From consultation to final placement, the entire process is straightforward and comfortable.

01

Consultation & Smile Design

We discuss your goals, review photos, and evaluate your teeth. We'll use shade guides to identify your ideal porcelain color and walk you through exactly what your new smile will look like before any work begins.

1 visit · ~60 min
02

Tooth Preparation

A thin layer of enamel (typically less than 0.5mm) is gently removed from each tooth receiving a veneer. Local anesthesia keeps you comfortable. Impressions are taken and sent to our dental lab to fabricate your custom veneers.

1 visit · local anesthesia
03

Temporaries & Lab Fabrication

While your permanent veneers are crafted by a skilled dental lab, we place temporary veneers so you're never without a smile. Most labs take 1–2 weeks to complete your custom porcelain pieces.

1–2 weeks · lab turnaround
04

Final Veneer Placement

Your permanent veneers are tried in, color-checked, and bonded with dental-grade adhesive. We make any last-minute adjustments and cure the bond. You leave with a brand-new smile same day.

1 visit · final result
Know Your Options

Porcelain vs. Composite Veneers

Both types of veneers can transform your smile but they differ in longevity, process, and cost. Here's what you need to know to make the right choice.

💎

Porcelain Veneers

  • ✓Lifespan: 15+ years with normal care
  • ✓Appearance: Most natural, mirrors real enamel light refraction
  • ✓Stain resistance: Highly resistant to coffee, wine, and tobacco
  • ✓Visits: 2 appointments over 1–2 weeks
  • →Cost: Higher initial investment
☀️

Composite Veneers

  • ✓Lifespan: 5–7 years with proper maintenance
  • ✓Appearance: Good aesthetic result, slightly less translucent
  • ✓Repairability: Chips can often be repaired chair-side
  • ✓Visits: Often completed in 1 appointment (same day)
  • ✓Cost: More affordable starting point

Not sure which is right for you? We'll walk through both options at your consultation no pressure, just honest guidance.

The preparation appointment uses local anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Some patients experience mild sensitivity in the days following preparation especially to cold. With proper care, porcelain veneers typically last 15 years or more many patients get 20+ years from their veneers. The keys are good oral hygiene, wearing a night guard if you grind your teeth, and avoiding habits like biting your nails or chewing ice. Composite veneers have a shorter lifespan of 5–7 years.
