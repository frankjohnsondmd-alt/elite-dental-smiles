Why Drugstore Products Fall Short

Walk into any drugstore and you will find a whole shelf dedicated to whitening products. Strips, trays, toothpastes, pens. The problem is not that these products are total scams. Some do whiten teeth a shade or two. The issue is concentration. Over-the-counter products contain low-level peroxide, usually around 10 percent or less, because they are designed to be used without supervision.

Professional whitening uses much higher concentrations of hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide. The kind we use in office can lighten teeth several shades in a single visit. Our custom take-home trays use professional-grade gel that you simply cannot buy at a pharmacy.

There is also the fit issue. Generic whitening trays do not conform to your teeth. Gel leaks onto your gums, which causes irritation and means less of the whitening agent actually contacts your enamel. Custom trays made from impressions of your mouth solve that problem completely.

Who Is a Good Candidate for Whitening?

Professional whitening works best on natural enamel that has been stained by coffee, tea, wine, tobacco, or just normal aging. Most healthy adults with those types of stains are excellent candidates.

There are situations where whitening alone may not give you the result you are hoping for. Crowns, veneers, and tooth-colored fillings do not respond to peroxide. If you have significant restorations on your front teeth, we may recommend a different approach to get an even result. We will always be upfront about this before you commit to treatment.

Teeth that are gray due to tetracycline staining or trauma are also more resistant to whitening. In those cases, options like porcelain veneers often give better outcomes. We will look at the nature of your staining at your consult and tell you exactly what you can realistically expect.

What About Sensitivity?

Sensitivity is the most common concern patients bring up. Some people do experience temporary sensitivity during or after whitening, usually lasting 24 to 48 hours. The key word is temporary.

We take a few steps to manage this. We start by assessing your baseline sensitivity level. For patients who run sensitive, we may recommend a lower concentration protocol or use a desensitizing treatment before whitening. We also send patients home with specific instructions about what to eat and drink in the days following treatment, which makes a real difference.

If you have walked away from whitening before because of sensitivity, do not assume it will always be that way. Come talk to us first.

How Long Do Results Last?

Professional whitening results last longer than anything you will get from a drugstore kit, but they are not permanent. How long your results hold depends largely on your habits.

Coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco are the biggest culprits for staining again. If you drink black coffee every morning, your results will fade faster than someone who does not. That said, with the custom take-home trays we provide, a quick touch-up every few months is easy and keeps your smile consistently bright.

Most patients get a full year or more out of their in-office treatment before they feel they need a significant touch-up. That is a much better return than repeating drugstore kits every few weeks for mediocre results.

Common Questions About Teeth Whitening

Does whitening damage enamel?

When done correctly, professional whitening does not damage enamel. The peroxide gel temporarily opens enamel pores to remove stains, then the enamel remineralizes on its own. We use concentrations and protocols that are safe for your enamel. The key is doing it under professional supervision, not overdoing it with repeated OTC products.

Can I whiten if I have cavities?

No. We always check for and treat any active decay before whitening. Peroxide on a cavity is painful and makes the decay worse. This is one more reason to start with a dental exam rather than buying something off a shelf.

What if my teeth are already pretty white?

Whitening can still improve the brightness and evenness of your smile even if you are starting from a decent baseline. Some patients are surprised by how much brighter they can go. We will give you an honest assessment at the consult.
